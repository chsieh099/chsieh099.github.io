Mounza UI/UX Design

In the summer of 2014, I interned at Mounza—a career networking company— as the Lead UI/UX Designer. During this time, I headed a team of programming interns to develop clean and navigable interfaces for Android, iOS, and web platforms.

I began redeveloping the mobile interface first. During this process, I distinguished the most important features of the app, namely the News Feed, Events, Profile, and Subscriptions pages.

Starting with the Events page, I played around with different styles, colors, and layouts. Here are three iterations of the Events page, though there were many iterations in between. The first is the original app interface, second one of my iterations, and the third the final design.

After deciding on an overall look and feel for the app, I drew up a design scheme for the rest of the pages. Below, I pulled from the finalized page for event descriptions to apply the same style to the other mobile pages.

The style was applied to the other pages and included as a part of the introduction when opening the app on your mobile device for the first time.

Upon completing the mobile pages, I moved on to apply the same brand identity to the web interface in order to keep the app consistent across all platforms.

During this internship, I not only mastered my Photoshop skills but developed a clearer understanding of how applications are integrated with university standards like SAML format and the shibboleth system.

My last task during my internship was to design a layout for weekly emails, providing the most attended event for the upcoming week, reminding users of upcoming events, and suggesting related events. I sought to create a light design scheme that would be more of a friendly update for users rather than a nagging reminder.
